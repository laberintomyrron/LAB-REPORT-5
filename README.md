# LAB-REPORT-5
This experiment explores the full demodulation of a Quadrature Phase Shift Keying (QPSK) signal. From verifying the operation of parallel to serial, serial to parallel to generating an QPSK signal to full demodulation of QPSK signal.

---

OBJECTIVES:

---

The primary objective of this laboratory is to conduct a full study of Quadrature Phase Shift Keying (QPSK) modulation and demodulation using the Emona Telecoms-Trainer 101. In this lab, we will first verify the operation of serial-to-parallel and parallel-to-serial conversion to observe how a single digital data stream can be split into two independent bit streams and successfully reconstructed back into its original form without any loss of data. Additionally, this laboratory is designed to investigate the generation of a QPSK signal by exploring how the two parallel bit streams are used to phase-modulate two orthogonal carriers. Another core objective is to model realistic channel conditions by introducing noise, band-limiting, and phase shifting to the transmitted QPSK signal, simulating the impairments that a signal would typically encounter when traveling through a real communication channel.
